To My Journey

Background

In 2004 I was introduced to Adobe products and coding HTML. I began working on a website to sell t-shirts. I used Photoshop to adjust images I pulled online. When I found that writing code allowed me to create layouts with my images and text, I thought I was off to the races. I was coding HTML, uploading my files to "godaddy.com" ftp accounts, adjusting images using Photoshop and Aftereffects. Only, I did not realize the significance of the skills I was learning or, better yet, how I could put those skill to work for me financially in the present and future.

I became a family man, marrying a beautiful woman and having two beautiful children, but unfortunately that meant "playing on my computer" had to take a backseat. I took a temporary assignment for maybe the largest HVAC company in the world as a dispatcher where I dispatched HVAC technicians nationwide. 10 years later I had risen to management for a small HVAC company in Baltimore. I never lost the urge to create and I always kept up with the latest coding languages and web development trends as well as freelancing for family and friends. I would always go the extra mile creating flyers, ads, writing programs, and creating spreadsheets using Microsoft Office products trying to make jobs easier for all employees.
